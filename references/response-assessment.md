# Author Response and Revised Manuscript Assessment

Use this reference when the user provides author responses, reviewer comments,
revision notes, a revised manuscript, or asks for second-round review.

## Assessment Questions

Check whether:

- the authors respond point by point;
- the manuscript was actually revised, not only explained in the response letter;
- added data, methods, figures, analyses, or text address the concern;
- major concerns are resolved;
- the response avoids key issues;
- weak explanations substitute for substantive revisions;
- new content introduces new problems;
- conclusions are more robust after revision;
- remaining problems require continued major revision;
- the manuscript can move to minor revision or acceptance.

## Response Assessment Table

Use:

| Reviewer concern | Author response | Manuscript revision | Assessment | Remaining issue |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Assessment labels:

- resolved
- partly resolved
- not resolved
- unclear from provided material
- new issue introduced

## Recommendation After Revision

Choose:

- Accept
- Minor revision
- Major revision
- Reject

Base the recommendation on the unresolved issues, not on the authors' tone.

## Wording Patterns

```text
The authors have partly addressed this concern by adding [revision]. However, the revised manuscript still does not fully resolve [remaining issue]. In particular, [specific gap] remains unclear, and this affects the robustness of [conclusion]. I recommend that the authors further [specific action].
```

```text
The response clarifies the authors' intention, but the manuscript text still lacks the evidence needed to support the conclusion. The authors should incorporate the key explanation into the Methods/Results/Discussion and provide the relevant quantitative analysis rather than only explaining it in the response letter.
```

```text
This concern has been adequately addressed. The added analysis/text improves the clarity of [aspect], and no further major revision appears necessary on this point.
```
