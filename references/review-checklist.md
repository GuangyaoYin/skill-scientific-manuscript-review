# General Scientific Review Checklist

Use this checklist for complete manuscripts, abstracts, sections, figures, and
draft review comments. Keep the review anchored in the claim-evidence chain.

## Manuscript Information

Extract when available:

- Title
- Target journal
- Research field
- Study area
- Research object
- Data type
- Methodological framework
- Main results
- Main conclusions
- Claimed novelty
- Claimed contribution
- Potential controversy
- Key figures and tables
- Key references
- Strongest part
- Weakest part

Suggested summary block:

```text
Manuscript information:
- Title:
- Field:
- Study area:
- Data:
- Methods:
- Main conclusions:
- Claimed novelty:
- Overall contribution:
- Potential concerns:
```

## Scientific Question

Check whether:

- the scientific question is explicit;
- the introduction identifies a real knowledge gap;
- question, methods, results, and discussion are aligned;
- the paper avoids a "large question, small evidence" mismatch;
- the work is more than a descriptive data report;
- title, abstract, and conclusion do not overstate significance;
- the question fits the target journal.

Output as:

- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Reviewer comment
- Suggested revision

## Novelty

Classify novelty:

- High novelty
- Moderate novelty
- Limited novelty
- Insufficient novelty

Distinguish:

- data novelty
- methodological novelty
- mechanistic novelty
- regional geological or geophysical insight
- application value
- practical significance

Check whether the authors merely claim "first application in this area", compare
adequately with prior work, omit key studies, exaggerate novelty, or truly change
existing understanding.

## Data Quality

Check whether:

- data source is clear;
- temporal and spatial coverage are sufficient;
- resolution fits the research question;
- preprocessing and selection criteria are explained;
- completeness and reproducibility are assessed;
- missing data are discussed;
- uncertainty is quantified.

If data quality cannot support the central conclusion, mark it as a major concern
or critical issue.

## Method Reliability

Check whether:

- methods fit the question;
- assumptions are stated;
- parameters and thresholds are justified;
- sensitivity analysis and error propagation are included;
- independent validation is provided;
- comparison with existing methods is adequate;
- the paper avoids circular reasoning;
- quantitative validation replaces purely experience-based judgment;
- details are sufficient for reproducibility.

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Check whether:

- results answer the scientific question;
- figures/tables support text claims;
- statistical relationships are significant and properly interpreted;
- evidence is selectively displayed;
- observations, model results, and interpretations are separated;
- uncertainty ranges are given;
- alternative explanations are discussed;
- conflicts with prior work are explained.

Generate a major comment when a core conclusion lacks direct evidence.

## Causality

Check whether authors overinterpret:

- temporal correlation;
- spatial correlation;
- statistical correlation;
- visual correspondence;
- model plausibility.

Assess the causal chain:

1. disturbance exists;
2. disturbance precedes the response;
3. distance and geometry are plausible;
4. disturbance can propagate to the source region;
5. magnitude is sufficient to affect stability;
6. seismicity shows expected migration or response;
7. natural tectonic explanations are considered;
8. model or independent evidence supports the interpretation;
9. alternative mechanisms are discussed.

Flag overstrong words such as `prove`, `demonstrate`, `confirm`, `control`,
`dominate`, `cause`, `trigger`, `induce`, `directly reflect`, and
`strongly indicate` when evidence is insufficient.

Prefer softer wording such as `suggest`, `imply`, `may indicate`,
`are consistent with`, `could be related to`, and `potentially associated with`.

## Uncertainty and Sensitivity

Check whether:

- key parameters have uncertainty ranges;
- threshold choices affect conclusions;
- data errors propagate to results;
- model parameters are tested;
- classification proportions include confidence intervals;
- conclusions depend on one assumption;
- robustness tests or alternative models are included;
- limitations are acknowledged.

Reusable comment:

```text
The uncertainty associated with [parameter/data/model assumption] is not quantified. Because the main conclusion depends strongly on [specific assumption], the authors should perform sensitivity tests and provide uncertainty ranges for the reported results.
```

## Discussion, Conclusion, Figures, References, Language

Discussion should explain mechanisms, compare with prior work, discuss
alternative interpretations, define applicability, acknowledge limitations, and
respond to the introduction's scientific question.

Conclusions should come directly from results, avoid overgeneralization, avoid
turning inference into certainty, and remain consistent with abstract and
discussion.

Figures should be readable, self-contained, correctly labelled, consistent with
text, and supported by captions. Statistical figures should include sample size,
time span, and uncertainty when relevant.

References should include classic and recent work, avoid inappropriate support,
avoid excessive self-citation, and include opposing or alternative explanations
when relevant.

Language review should check abstract logic, reproducible methods, restrained
results, non-repetitive discussion, concise conclusions, term consistency,
grammar, figure citation order, symbols, units, and abbreviations.

## Issue Severity

Critical issue:

- scientific question is invalid;
- method has a fundamental error;
- data cannot support the central conclusion;
- conclusion is seriously overstated;
- main analysis must be redone.

Major concern:

- study has value but central evidence chain is incomplete;
- key validation is missing;
- sensitivity or uncertainty analysis is missing;
- key parameters lack justification;
- important interpretation is overextended.

Minor comment:

- local explanation is insufficient;
- figure caption is incomplete;
- references need supplementation;
- language needs improvement;
- format or terminology needs consistency.

Editorial suggestion:

- spelling, punctuation, captions, layout, units, and formatting.
